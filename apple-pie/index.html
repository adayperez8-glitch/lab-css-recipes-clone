Apple
Pie

My grandmother's recipe. I've never seen another quite like it — and it's won me first place in more than a few local competitions.

30' Prep time
50' Cook time
8 Servings
Prep Time 30 minutes
Cook Time 50 minutes
Total 80 minutes
Servings 8 slices
Difficulty Medium

What you need

Ingredients

Pastry for a 9-inch double crust pie
½ cup unsalted butter
3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
¼ cup water
½ cup white sugar
½ cup packed brown sugar
8 Granny Smith apples — peeled, cored and sliced

Step by step

How to make it

1

Prepare the caramel sauce

Preheat oven to 425 °F (220 °C). Melt the butter in a saucepan. Stir in flour to form a paste. Add water, white sugar and brown sugar, and bring to a boil. Reduce temperature and let simmer.

2

Assemble the pie

Place the bottom crust in your pan. Fill with apples, mounded slightly. Cover with a lattice-work crust. Gently pour the sugar and butter liquid over the crust — pour slowly so that it does not run off.

3

Bake to perfection

Bake 15 minutes in the preheated oven. Reduce the temperature to 350 °F (175 °C). Continue baking for 35 to 45 minutes, until the apples are soft and the crust is golden brown.

💡
Pro Tip

Cover the edges of the crust with foil during the first 15 minutes to prevent over-browning, then remove it for the final bake to get a perfectly even golden finish.
